# AI Literacy

## Definition
The ability to understand, evaluate, and effectively interact with AI systems — encompassing both technical awareness (what AI can and cannot do) and practical judgment (when to trust, question, or override AI output).

## My Practice
(Compiled from origin: self sources)

- Developed an "AI Says / I Say" classroom format for 10-11 year olds that builds functional AI skepticism without requiring technical knowledge of how LLMs work
- Found that skepticism transfers: students began questioning textbooks and teacher explanations using the same frameworks
- High achievers struggled more — their trust in authoritative text made them less skeptical of AI output
- Assessment challenge: hard to distinguish genuine critical thinking from performed skepticism (students modeling what the teacher values)

## External Perspectives
(Compiled from origin: external sources)

- **Anthropic (2025)**: Defines AI fluency as a 4-level spectrum (Awareness → Familiarity → Proficiency → Fluency). Emphasizes that fluency means redesigning work processes, not just using AI tools
- **UNESCO (2025)**: Proposes a competency framework centered on ethical use and societal impact, less focused on individual skill development
- **Mollick (2025)**: Frames AI interaction as "co-intelligence" — the skill is knowing when to delegate to AI and when to override it. Primarily addresses knowledge workers, not students

## Tensions & Gaps
- My classroom experience suggests AI literacy is possible without technical understanding, but most external frameworks assume technical awareness as a prerequisite — who's right?
- UNESCO emphasizes ethical/societal dimensions that my classroom practice barely touches — is functional skepticism enough, or do students need to understand bias, privacy, and labor implications?
- All external frameworks target adults or teenagers. Evidence for elementary-age AI literacy is almost nonexistent
- "Fluency" implies a stable target, but AI capabilities shift quarterly. Is AI literacy a moving goalpost by design?

## Examples
- A 10-year-old student said: "The AI sounds sure, but it didn't actually answer my question" — demonstrating evaluation without technical knowledge
- Anthropic's case study: A history teacher who redesigned essays to require critiquing AI arguments
- UNESCO pilot in Finland: National AI literacy curriculum for secondary schools
