# Anthropic's AI Fluency Framework for Educators

## Core Conclusion
AI fluency goes beyond knowing how to prompt — it requires understanding what AI can and cannot do, recognizing when AI output needs human judgment, and developing the taste to evaluate AI-generated work. Anthropic frames this as a spectrum from awareness to fluency, not a binary skill.

## Key Evidence
- Their framework defines 4 levels: Awareness → Familiarity → Proficiency → Fluency
- Teachers at the "Fluency" level don't just use AI — they redesign assignments knowing students have access to it
- The course emphasizes "AI as thought partner" over "AI as answer machine"
- Case study: A history teacher redesigned essay prompts to require students to critique AI-generated arguments rather than write from scratch

## Open Questions
- The framework was developed primarily with US educators — unclear how it transfers to different educational cultures and age groups
- "Fluency" as the top level implies mastery is possible, but AI capabilities change faster than any fluency framework can track
- No mention of how to assess AI fluency in students (only in teachers)

## Key Terms
- **AI Fluency**: The ability to effectively collaborate with AI tools while maintaining critical judgment about their outputs
- **Prompt Literacy**: A subset of AI fluency focused on crafting effective inputs — necessary but not sufficient
